About Commercial Property Cleaning

Commercial Property Cleaning (CPC) is a national commercial cleaning company operating across government, council, industrial, education, office tower and other regulated sectors in Australia. CPC is distinguished by its ISO-certified management systems, verified carbon neutral program, modern slavery compliance documentation and permanent state-based operational teams across five states.

What We Do

CPC delivers commercial cleaning services to regulated, complex and compliance-driven environments. Our clients include state and federal government agencies, local councils, industrial operators, education portfolios, commercial property managers and national corporate clients. CPC approaches cleaning as a compliance function — not a labour commodity — and our management systems, reporting tools and workforce governance reflect that positioning.

How We Operate

CPC operates with permanent local teams in Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria, Western Australia and South Australia, supported by centralised contract management, national management systems and digital audit infrastructure. Local teams provide responsiveness and community knowledge. Central systems provide governance consistency, portfolio-level reporting and compliance management across all jurisdictions.

A single national account management model means that clients managing multi-site or multi-state portfolios have one point of contact, one reporting format and one compliance documentation source regardless of the geographic scope of their portfolio.

Our Management Philosophy

CPC treats cleaning as an operational control — a compliance function that requires documented systems, trained personnel, performance evidence and continuous improvement — rather than a commodity service managed primarily on price. This philosophy drives our investment in ISO certification, digital audit tools, modern slavery compliance systems, carbon neutral programs and workforce governance.

It also drives our approach to client relationships: long-term partnerships built on transparent performance evidence, proactive compliance management and genuine sector expertise — rather than transactional price competition that produces short-term wins and long-term governance risk for clients.

Careers at CPC

CPC is a national employer operating across government, industrial, education and commercial sectors in Australia. We offer structured training pathways, career progression and a professional working environment for people who want to build a career in facilities and cleaning management — not just fill a shift.

Why Work at CPC

CPC is a compliance-driven organisation — which means our workforce is taken seriously. We invest in training, credential management, safety systems and career development because the quality of our workforce is the primary determinant of our contract performance. We offer award-compliant employment with correct entitlements, genuine training pathways from operative to supervisor to contract manager, and the stability of a national employer with a diversified client portfolio across multiple sectors and states.

CPC's ISO-certified management systems create a professional working environment with documented processes, clear expectations and structured performance management. Our people know what is expected of them, have the training and equipment to deliver it, and are managed by supervisors who are trained to support their development as well as their performance.

Our Commitment to Our Workforce

CPC pays award-compliant wages with correct entitlements — including penalty rates, leave entitlements and superannuation — as a fundamental operating principle, not an aspiration. Right-to-work verification is a standard pre-employment step. Grievance mechanisms are accessible and functional. Modern slavery compliance is integrated into our workforce management rather than treated as a compliance document separate from operations.

CPC is an equal opportunity employer. We actively recruit from diverse backgrounds and have Indigenous employment partnerships as part of our social procurement commitments.

Social Procurement

CPC's social procurement commitments are genuine operational practices — not tender-time commitments assembled from aspirational policies. Indigenous employment partnerships, social enterprise supplier relationships and community impact activities are integrated into CPC's ongoing operations and documented for procurement evaluation and reporting purposes.

Why Social Procurement Matters in Cleaning

Commercial cleaning is a labour-intensive sector that employs people from diverse backgrounds across all geographic areas of Australia — including regional and remote communities where employment opportunities are limited. A cleaning company with genuine social procurement commitments can create meaningful employment and economic participation for Indigenous Australians and people from disadvantaged backgrounds at scale, not as a pilot program.

For government procurement teams evaluating social procurement requirements — whether under the Commonwealth Indigenous Procurement Policy, state-level social procurement policies or tender-specific social value criteria — CPC's documented social procurement evidence provides substantive material for evaluation, not just policy statements.

Indigenous Employment Partnerships

CPC maintains active Indigenous employment partnerships that create pathways for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people into employment, training and career development in the cleaning sector. These partnerships are maintained year-round and documented with employment outcomes, training completions and career progression evidence. CPC's commitment to reconciliation is reflected in operational practice rather than symbolic statement.

Social Enterprise Supplier Relationships

CPC maintains commercial relationships with social enterprise suppliers for cleaning products, consumables and ancillary services where quality requirements are met and commercial terms are viable. These relationships are documented with supplier descriptions, procurement volumes and social impact data that can be provided to clients requiring supply chain social enterprise evidence for their own procurement reporting obligations.

Community Impact Reporting

CPC documents community impact outcomes associated with its social procurement activities, including employment hours created for disadvantaged community members, training completions and qualifications achieved by Indigenous employees, procurement spend with social enterprise suppliers, and community engagement activities. This data is provided to government clients requiring supply chain social impact reporting for procurement acquittal and program evaluation.

Local Content Commitments

Where CPC operates in regional and remote areas — including far north Queensland, regional Western Australia and other areas with local content expectations in government procurement — CPC prioritises employment of local community members, engagement of local subcontractors and suppliers where quality and cost requirements are met, and investment in local training and development.

Commonwealth Indigenous Procurement Policy

The Commonwealth Indigenous Procurement Policy (IPP) requires Commonwealth Government agencies to consider Indigenous enterprises and employment in their procurement activities. CPC's Indigenous employment and enterprise engagement practices are documented to support client agencies in meeting their IPP reporting and activity requirements through their cleaning contracts.

CPC Management Approach

CPC's contract management approach is designed to provide clients with transparent performance evidence, proactive compliance management and responsive escalation — rather than reactive problem resolution triggered by client complaints. This page describes how CPC structures and governs cleaning contracts from mobilisation through to performance review.

Mobilisation Process

Contract mobilisation begins before service commencement with three parallel workstreams: workforce mobilisation (credential verification, site inductions, deployment planning), systems configuration (audit tool setup for site-specific requirements, reporting format alignment, KPI threshold confirmation), and compliance documentation delivery (ISO certificates, insurance, modern slavery statement and any contract-specific documentation required within the mobilisation period).

CPC's standard mobilisation commitment is to have full compliance documentation delivered and all required credentials completed within five business days of contract commencement — not within the first inspection period. Compliance readiness from day one is a contractual commitment, not an aspiration.

Account Management Structure

Each CPC contract has a nominated contract manager who is the single point of contact for the client facility or portfolio. For national or multi-state contracts, a national account manager sits above state contract managers and holds accountability for portfolio-level performance and client relationship management. The account management structure is documented at mobilisation — clients are never uncertain about who to call when an issue arises.

Digital Audit and Reporting

CPC uses a digital inspection platform across all contracts as standard — not as an optional add-on. Inspections capture timestamped, geolocated photographic evidence of completed areas, defect identification with severity classification, and rectification assignments with completion verification. Clients receive regular performance reports in the format required by their governance obligations — weekly, monthly or quarterly as specified in the contract.

The digital audit system eliminates the information asymmetry that paper-based inspection records create. Clients have access to the same performance data CPC uses internally, presented in a format that supports their own governance and reporting requirements without requiring manual data compilation.

Defect Management

When a defect is identified — whether through CPC's scheduled audit program, a client report or a supervisor inspection — it is recorded in CPC's digital system with a severity classification, assigned to the responsible supervisor and tracked to verified resolution. Defect response time commitments are specified in the contract and monitored against performance data. Persistent defect patterns — the same area failing repeatedly — trigger an investigation and corrective action process rather than continued reactive rectification.

Escalation Procedures

CPC's escalation framework has defined levels that match the severity and urgency of the issue. Operational issues (single-shift performance deficiency) are resolved by the site supervisor within the contracted response time. Contract management issues (persistent performance pattern, compliance documentation requirement) are managed by the contract manager. Strategic issues (contract-level performance concern, significant compliance event) are escalated to the national account manager and CPC's senior leadership. Clients always know which level of CPC leadership is accountable for managing their issue.

Performance Review Process

CPC conducts regular formal performance review meetings with clients — monthly for active contract management, quarterly as a minimum for stable contracts. Review meetings are based on performance data — audit scores, defect records, attendance data — not anecdotal discussion. Meetings produce a written record with agreed actions, accountability owners and completion timeframes that form part of the contract management file.
